🔄 The Annual Sweep: Your 5-Year Play

One phone call. One setup. Then your paycheque does the rest — year after year.

1
Call Manulife today and ask to set up a new 3-year fixed sub-account Tell them: "I want to lock a portion of my Main Account balance into a new fixed sub-account at the 3-year rate." They'll walk you through the amounts. This is a free service with your M1 account.
2
Lock most of your HELOC balance into the fixed sub-account Your Main Account has ~$247,500 available. Lock the majority into a 3-year fixed rate (lower than 4.95% variable). Leave approximately $51,600 in the HELOC — exactly one year's worth of your $4,300/month surplus.
3
Let your paycheque wipe out the HELOC portion by December Your $4,300/month surplus flows through the Main Account. The $51,600 HELOC balance gets paid to $0 in 12 months. You're not even thinking about it — it happens automatically through your income deposits.
4
Rinse and repeat — every year Once the HELOC hits zero, your fixed sub-account has also been amortizing. The room that's opened up gets swept into the next annual cycle. Every year, you eliminate another chunk. Every year, you get closer to free.
5
At your 3-year fixed renewal — accelerate again In 3 years, your fixed sub-account renews. At that point, you reassess the remaining balance and set up the next 3-year lock. By years 5–6, you're done. That's your retirement. That's your freedom.

The Power of the Paycheque — Simply
Why putting your salary into M1 matters so much
1
Your TDSB paycheque deposits directly into your Manulife One account. The moment it lands — whether it's $4,000 or $8,000 — that money is immediately reducing your balance. Interest charges that day are lower. Tomorrow's interest is lower. It adds up every single day.
2
Your bills, condo fees, and expenses flow out normally. Treat M1 exactly like your current chequing account. Pay everything from it. The difference is that in between pay and spend, your money is working against your debt instead of earning 0.5% at a regular bank.
3
At month's end, your surplus stays in the account. You don't need to do anything. The $4,300 that wasn't spent is sitting there, reducing tomorrow's interest automatically. No manual transfers. No extra effort.
4
Your $200,000 in savings is your secret weapon. Any savings you keep inside the M1 account kills interest on whatever HELOC balance you're carrying. You don't have to put all of it in — but even a portion makes a significant difference, especially in the early years.
