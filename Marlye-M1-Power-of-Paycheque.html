💰"Considering the fees, the penalty, the balance going from $305K to $312K — do I still come out ahead?"

🤔"Does it matter that the M1 rate is 4.95% when I could get 4.1% elsewhere?"
Great question — and this is the key insight: it's not about the rate, it's about how long money sits there accumulating interest. Your paycheque goes straight into the Main Account and immediately reduces your balance. Money doesn't sit idle overnight the way it does in a traditional account. The fewer days your balance is high, the less interest you're charged — even if the posted rate is slightly higher. A 4.95% rate on a balance racing toward zero beats 4.1% on a balance barely moving. And your two sub-accounts are locked at 4.74% — actually lower than what the Manulife calculator projected (4.94%) — so you'll do even better than the numbers show.
🔄"The $37,000 in the Main Account — will it ever go to zero?"
Yes — and that's the whole goal. You called it a washing machine, and that's exactly right. Your bi-weekly paycheque deposits into the Main Account → balance drops → you spend on living expenses → the surplus stays in, keeping the balance lower. Every day the balance is lower, you pay less interest. Over 12 months, your surplus attacks the $37,000 until it hits zero. When it does, you sweep that freed-up room as a lump sum toward Sub-account 2 — which shrinks faster. Then the cycle starts again.
📊"Where do the sub-account payments go? How does it all fit together?"
Your two sub-accounts ($497/mo and $1,840/mo) are separate, fixed scheduled payments that come out automatically every month. They pay down Sub-1 and Sub-2 on their own amortization schedule. Meanwhile, your paycheque deposits work against the Main Account $37,000 HELOC simultaneously. Three tracks running in parallel. The sub-accounts shrink on schedule. The Main Account shrinks faster every time your pay hits. You don't have to do anything — just keep your everyday banking inside Manulife One.
➕"Does this assume I make extra payments — or is it just what I already pay?"
No extra payments required. The Manulife calculator uses your existing confirmed income ($6,000/mo net) and your existing living expenses ($2,200/mo). The $12,000 prepayment you already made is what brought the CMLS balance down — that work is done. Going forward, it's your regular paycheque doing the heavy lifting. You don't write any extra cheques. You just redirect your direct deposit to Manulife One and the math takes over.
🏦"If I retire before the mortgage is paid off, can I still access my equity?"
This is exactly why doing this now matters. Once you're retired, your income is fixed — pension, CPP, OAS. Banks limit how much they'll lend based on that fixed income, and it's often far less than you'd need. Right now, Manulife has confirmed your salary at $126,962/year and is approving $390,000 in secured credit. Once this account is open, the HELOC room you create by paying down Sub-2 stays available — you don't have to requalify to use it.
