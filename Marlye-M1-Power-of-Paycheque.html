Why Sub-Account 1 is Non-Readvanceable

Manulife requires that 15% of the appraised value ($58,500) sit in a fixed, non-readvanceable account. This is a regulatory rule — not a penalty. Payments on this account permanently reduce your credit limit. The remaining 65% ($241,500) is fully readvanceable, which is where the strategy lives.

What is "The Power of the Paycheque"?

It's a simple idea: your mortgage charges interest every single day. If your money sits idle in a chequing account earning nothing, you're losing ground. Manulife One fixes that.

The Core Idea

When you deposit your paycheque into your Manulife One Main Account, that money instantly reduces the balance you're being charged interest on — from the very first day. You don't need to make an extra payment or remember to transfer anything. Your income does the work just by sitting there.

A Simple Example

Imagine your LOC balance is $12,000 at 4.95%.

Without M1
Your $3,000 paycheque sits in a chequing account earning ~0.1%.

Meanwhile, your $12,000 LOC charges you 4.95% every day.

Daily interest: $1.63/day
With M1
Your $3,000 paycheque deposits into the Main Account.

Balance becomes $9,000. Interest is now charged on $9,000.

Daily interest: $1.22/day — you saved $0.41 that day, automatically.
That $0.41/day adds up. Over a month, over a year, compounding daily — it becomes real money. And that's just on the $12,000 LOC. Your sub-accounts work the same way through the prepayment cycle described below.

The Annual Paycheque Cycle

1
Every month: Paycheque deposits into the Main Account
Your income lands in the M1 Main Account (LOC). Your regular expenses come out — mortgage payments, condo fees, groceries, bills. The leftover stays in the LOC and reduces your balance every single day.
2
Monthly surplus accumulates — ~$750 to $1,000/month
Every month you don't spend your full income, that surplus chips away at the $12,000 LOC balance. While it's sitting there, it's saving you ~4.95% interest per year — far better than any savings account.
3
Once a year: ~$12,000 has been paid into the LOC
By the end of 12 months, your surplus has paid down roughly $12,000 of the LOC balance. The LOC room is now available again — this is the key moment.
4
Apply the lump sum: Draw $12,000 from LOC → prepay Sub-Account 2
You re-advance $12,000 from the LOC and apply it as a lump-sum prepayment against Sub-Account 2 ($241,500 readvanceable). This directly reduces the principal you're paying 4.74% interest on — and re-opens that $12,000 of LOC room for the next cycle.
↺
Repeat every year — the compound effect grows
Each year the strategy runs, your sub-account balance is lower, which means lower monthly payments, which means a slightly larger surplus the next year. The cycle compounds and accelerates automatically over time.

The Paycheque Cycle in One Sentence

Paycheque → Main Account → Surplus reduces LOC balance → Annual lump sum prepayment on Sub-Account 2 → Repeat.

You don't need a big LOC for this to work. You just need the LOC to be sized to your cash flow — and for you, that's $12,000.

What You Need to Do — Your Simple Action Plan

The strategy runs almost automatically once it's set up. Here's your checklist.

  • Set up direct deposit. Have your employer deposit your paycheque directly into the Manulife One Main Account. This is the single most important step.
  • Pay all expenses from the Main Account. Use the Manulife One debit card for everyday spending and bills. The more you cycle through the account, the more interest you save.
  • Don't let the LOC go positive. If your balance goes above $0, that money is sitting idle instead of working. Keep it at $0 or below (i.e., drawing on the LOC to pay expenses is fine — that's the point).
  • Once a year: execute the sweep. When your LOC balance has been paid down by ~$12,000, re-advance that $12,000 and apply it as a prepayment on Sub-Account 2. You can do this online or with a phone call to Manulife Bank at 1-877-765-2265.
  • Renew and review at 5 years. Your term ends in June 2031. At renewal, Sub-Account 2 will have significantly less owing — because of the annual prepayments. Review your rate and consider accelerating further.

You Don't Have to Be Perfect

If you have a month where expenses are higher and there's no surplus — that's fine. The strategy doesn't break. You simply don't make that month's contribution to the LOC. The next month, you pick up where you left off. Consistency over time is what creates the result, not perfection every month.
